Mofongo

Description

Mofongo is a popular Puerto Rican side dish made with fried green plantains mashed by hand together with chicharrones (crispy pork skin) and fresh garlic. It’s traditionally served with stews, sauces, or broth, and makes for a super delicious main or side. It’s traditionally served with stews, sauces, or broth, and makes for a super delicious main or side. Because of the crispy texture from the pork rinds, it’s best enjoyed fresh!

Ingredients

  • Green Plantains
  • Garlic
  • Chicharrones
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Heat your oil in a heavy bottomed frying pan over medium heat
  2. Peal plantains while oil is heating and cut them into small slices
  3. Once oil is hot, gently add slices and fry until golden brown
  4. Stil occasionally so they cook evenly
  5. Once cooked, remove them from the oil and dry them on a paper towel lined plate
  6. In a large bowl or a mortar and pestel, add garlic and chicharrones and mash.
  7. Add the frined plantain slices and mash them in with the other ingredients
  8. To plate, fill a small bowl with the mashed plantains flip into a plate and there you have Mofongo!
